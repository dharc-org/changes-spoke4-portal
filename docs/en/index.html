IT

The project was born within the framework of the national CHANGES initiative (Spoke 4), with the aim of promoting the structured digitization of cultural heritage with a scientific vocation. The case study dedicated to Ulisse Aldrovandi integrates 3D technologies, advanced metadata and digital communication strategies to build replicable models of cultural digital twins. The platform is designed for researchers, curators, students and cultural institutions interested in tools for data consultation, analysis and storytelling.

(Phase 1)
Collection and organization of cultural data
  • Acquisition and modelling of 3D objects and environments
  • Collection of metadata from museums, galleries and archives
  • Mapping of pre-existing sources
(Phase 2)
Metadata and standardization
  • Convergence and harmonization of metadata according to European standards
  • Design of application profiles (e.g. CHAD-AP)
  • Validation and semantic enrichment of data
Creation of digital prototypes
  • Design of interactive models (Data Stories)
  • Software and interface development for access
  • Integration with real-time data and metadata
(Phase 3)
Dissemination and valorization
  • Communication strategies and digital storytelling
  • Creation of data stories and open access platforms
  • Engagement of universities, museums, schools and researchers

Objects by Type

Over 200 objects catalogued, digitised and partly modelled in 3D form a solid basis for semantic querying and the construction of new scientific and cultural narratives.
